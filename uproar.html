Uproar

Introduction

I created Uproar for a teacher profile design challenge. My goal was to have Uproar offered as a G Suite application to make it easier for teachers to communicate and collaborate.

Research

My research was limited because I was only given 4 days to complete this design challenge. Also, there is not a lot of teacher profiles out there to conduct competitor research in the first place. Luckily, I was a teacher for several years, so I deceided to interview several of my teacher collegules to find out the answers to the following questions:

1. How much of a social media presence do teacher want?

2. Who would be able to see a teacher's profile?

3. What profile features would be beneficial to teachers?

4. How can teachers collaborate with G Suite apps such as Google Slides and Google Docs?

5. How can teachers share lessons?

What I Discovered

I found that most teachers did not want another social media platform. However, many did say that they wanted to be able to like another teacher's lessons and have some form of messaging on the app. Most teachers wanted their school collegues to be able to video their profiles, but some teachers wanted to share their profiles with the enitre disrict or even everyone on the platform (this would give them access to more lessons from a variety of different schools and districts). To accomodate everyone's needs, I decided to make it up to the user to choose who can view their profile by manipulating their user settings. Interview participants stated that they wanted a way to organize their lessons by units and easily see and share lessons and units within their departments.

Ideation

Due to the time constraint, I decided not to do personas for this project. Instead, I created a user flow based on the information I received from the interviews. I developed seven screens to accomodate teacher's needs: home, community, activity, school, district, messages, and profile settings.


Wireframe

I quickly sketched out what features needed to be included on each page. I wanted teachers to be able to quickly access their notofications and see which lessons they were creating with their collegues, so I decided to add these features to the home page. The school page includes the school's administrators profiles and the school's teachers profiles. Teachers will be organized by department and users will be able to search for specific teachers by department name or teacher name. The profile page will show how many lessons the teacher has created, information about the teacher, and a message button for easy communication. The profile page will also show the teacher's most popular lessons (most likes and most adds), the teacher's public units, his/her most recent lessons, and the teacher's department. The district page, will contain all of the administrator's and teacher's profiles in the district. Finally, the profile settings page will give the user the ability, to set privacy settings to their profiles, lessons, and units.

Design

My design goal was to create something fun and playful, but will also be easy to incorporate other G Suite apps.


Prototype

User testing still needs to be conducted in order to improve the application. There was no time to conduct user testing within the four day span of this project.

Conclusion

User testings needs to be conducted in order to make improvements and additions the Uproar.
